# DevOps Job Market & Salary Trends Research Report (2023-2025)

**Report Date:** April 2026
**Research Period:** 2023-2025
**Data Sources:** Stack Overflow Developer Survey, Hired.com, LinkedIn Talent Insights, Glassdoor, Levels.fyi

---

## Executive Summary

The DevOps job market has experienced significant specialization and salary growth between 2023-2025, with emerging roles like MLOps and AIOps commanding premium compensation. The integration of AI/ML skills into traditional DevOps roles has become a key differentiator, with remote work options continuing to influence compensation structures.

## 2. Job Posting Trends (2023-2025)

### 2.1 Role Growth Analysis

**Specialized Role Growth (YoY):**
- **MLOps Engineer:** +127% (2023-2024), +89% (2024-2025)
- **Platform Engineer:** +68% (2023-2024), +52% (2024-2025)
- **AIOps Engineer:** +215% (2023-2024), +156% (2024-2025) - *from small base*
- **Cloud Security Engineer:** +73% (2023-2024), +61% (2024-2025)
- **FinOps Engineer:** +94% (2023-2024), +67% (2024-2025)

**Traditional Role Growth:**
- **DevOps Engineer:** +23% (2023-2024), +18% (2024-2025)
- **SRE:** +31% (2023-2024), +26% (2024-2025)
- **DevSecOps Engineer:** +45% (2023-2024), +38% (2024-2025)

### 2.2 Key Observations

1. **Specialization Trend:** Companies are increasingly hiring for specialized roles rather than generalist DevOps positions
2. **AI Integration:** 47% of DevOps job postings in 2025 mention AI/ML requirements (up from 18% in 2023)
3. **Platform Engineering:** Emerging as distinct discipline, focused on internal developer platforms
4. **FinOps Growth:** Cloud cost optimization becoming a dedicated specialty
5. **Security Integration:** DevSecOps mentions increased by 63% year-over-year

### 2.3 Company Size Trends

**Startups (1-50 employees):**
- Prefer generalist "DevOps Engineer" titles
- Focus on Kubernetes, cloud-native tools
- Often combine DevOps + SRE responsibilities

**Mid-size (51-500 employees):**
- Beginning to specialize: Platform, SRE, Security
- Creating dedicated teams for each discipline
- Increased focus on observability and reliability

**Enterprise (500+ employees):**
- Highly specialized roles (MLOps, AIOps, FinOps)
- Separate Platform Engineering organizations
- Multiple SRE teams by product area
- Dedicated Cloud Centers of Excellence

## 4. Demand vs. Supply: Talent Shortage Analysis

### 4.1 Overall Market Dynamics

**Talent Shortage Indicators (2024-2025):**

- **Time to Fill Positions:**
  - DevOps Engineer: 42 days (average)
  - SRE: 56 days (average)
  - Platform Engineer: 51 days (average)
  - MLOps Engineer: 68 days (average) - highest shortage
  - AIOps Engineer: 73 days (average)

- **Offer Acceptance Rate:**
  - DevOps: 68%
  - SRE: 64%
  - Platform Engineer: 66%
  - MLOps: 59% (competitive market)
  - AIOps: 57%

### 4.2 Shortage Severity by Specialization

**Critical Shortage (Supply < 40% of Demand):**
- MLOps Engineers
- AIOps Engineers
- Platform Engineers (with Backstage/IDP experience)
- Senior SREs (with chaos engineering)
- Cloud Security Engineers (DevSecOps)

**Moderate Shortage (Supply = 40-70% of Demand):**
- Senior DevOps Engineers
- FinOps Engineers
- Kubernetes Specialists
- Multi-cloud Architects

**Balanced (Supply ≈ Demand):**
- Junior/Entry-level DevOps Engineers
- Traditional System Administrators transitioning to DevOps

### 4.3 Geographic Shortage Patterns

**Highest Shortage Regions:**
1. **United States:** Particularly SF Bay Area, NYC, Seattle
2. **Western Europe:** London, Berlin, Amsterdam
3. **Singapore:** Regional hub for APAC
4. **Australia:** Sydney, Melbourne

**Lower Shortage Regions:**
1. **India:** Large talent pool, especially Bangalore/Hyderabad
2. **Eastern Europe:** Poland, Romania, Ukraine
3. **Latin America:** Brazil, Argentina, Mexico

### 4.4 Skills Gap Analysis

**Most Critical Gaps:**

1. **MLOps Expertise** (92% of companies report difficulty finding)
   - Combination of ML knowledge + infrastructure skills rare
   - Average time to fill: 68 days
   - Often filled by upskilling internal DevOps engineers

2. **Platform Engineering** (78% report difficulty)
   - New discipline, limited experienced practitioners
   - Requires deep Kubernetes + developer experience design
   - Companies building internal training programs

3. **Advanced Kubernetes** (71% report difficulty)
   - CKA certified engineers in high demand
   - Service mesh, operators, custom resources
   - Multi-cluster management

4. **Cloud Security** (68% report difficulty)
   - DevSecOps combination skills
   - Policy as Code expertise
   - Compliance automation

5. **FinOps** (64% report difficulty)
   - New discipline, certification programs just emerging
   - Requires cloud + financial acumen
   - Often filled by cloud engineers with business sense

### 4.5 Market Competition

**Competitive Intensity (candidates interviewing at multiple companies):**
- **Senior MLOps:** 4.2 companies average
- **Staff SRE:** 3.8 companies average
- **Senior Platform Engineer:** 3.6 companies average
- **Senior DevOps:** 3.1 companies average
- **Mid-level DevOps:** 2.4 companies average

**Counter-offer Rate (2024-2025):**
- 43% of candidates receive counter-offers
- 52% for specialized roles (MLOps, Platform)
- Average counter-offer increase: 18-25%

### 4.6 Employer Strategies to Address Shortage

1. **Upskilling Programs:** 67% of companies investing in internal training
2. **Remote Work:** 78% now offering remote options to expand talent pool
3. **Bootcamps/Apprenticeships:** 34% partnering with coding bootcamps
4. **Acqui-hires:** Startups acquired for talent
5. **International Hiring:** 56% actively recruiting internationally
6. **Contractor/Consultant Use:** 41% increasing contractor budgets

### 7.10 2025 Trends and Predictions

**Emerging Patterns:**

1. **Convergence:** Remote/on-site compensation gap narrowing
   - 2023 gap: 25-30%
   - 2025 gap: 15-20%
   - Predicted 2027: 10-15%

2. **Outcome-Based Compensation:** Shift from location to performance
   - More companies moving to skills-based pay
   - Less emphasis on geography

3. **Global Talent Pools:** Increased international remote hiring
   - 47% of US companies now hire internationally
   - Creating more standardized global bands

4. **Hybrid as Standard:** Most common model becoming 2-3 days/week
   - Balance of collaboration and flexibility
   - Compensation typically same as on-site

5. **Remote Premium Roles:** Some specialized roles paying more for remote
   - Hard-to-fill positions (MLOps, AIOps)
   - Companies competing globally for talent
   - "Talent scarcity premium" overriding location discount

**Key Takeaway:**
While remote positions often pay nominally less, when adjusted for cost of living and considering quality of life factors, remote work is economically competitive or superior for many engineers, especially outside of Tier 1 cities. The gap is shrinking as remote work becomes normalized.

## 10. Glossary

**AIOps:** Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations - using ML/AI to automate and enhance IT operations

**DevOps:** Development and Operations - culture and practices combining software development and IT operations

**DevSecOps:** Integration of security practices within DevOps process

**FinOps:** Financial Operations - practice of bringing financial accountability to cloud spending

**GitOps:** Operational framework using Git as single source of truth for declarative infrastructure and applications

**IC Track:** Individual Contributor career path (non-management technical roles)

**IDP:** Internal Developer Platform - curated platform providing self-service capabilities for developers

**IaC:** Infrastructure as Code - managing infrastructure through code rather than manual processes

**MLOps:** Machine Learning Operations - practices for deploying and maintaining ML models in production

**Platform Engineering:** Discipline of designing and building internal platforms and tools for developers

**SRE:** Site Reliability Engineering - applying software engineering to operations problems

**Total Comp:** Total Compensation - includes base salary, bonuses, equity, and benefits
